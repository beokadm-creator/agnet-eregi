## Design Context

### Users
Korean adults (primarily) navigating legal and administrative processes for the first time — real estate registration, court filings, administrative documents. They access the product at the start of a stressful process: something unfamiliar and consequential is happening in their lives. Mobile usage is common. They are not evaluating options — they need to be oriented and guided to act.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, trustworthy. Like a knowledgeable professional who handles the complexity quietly so the user doesn't have to feel it.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined minimalism. Light mode. Not a SaaS landing page — closer to the quiet reception of a trusted institution. Generous negative space. Every element earns its presence.

**Brand accent**: Deep forest green / jade — `oklch(36% 0.092 155)`. Reads as professional and trustworthy, not energetic. Far from the indigo/purple SaaS monoculture.

**Fonts**:
- Display / wordmark: Hahmlet (Korean serif, Google Fonts) — dignity and warmth without stiffness
- UI / body: Gothic A1 (Korean sans, Google Fonts) — maximum legibility at all sizes, many weights

**Anti-references**: The typical Korean fintech (aggressive CTA colors, packed with features, heavy use of blue), standard SaaS landing template, generic admin dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet authority** — let competence speak through precision, not volume. No bullet-point feature lists.
2. **Legibility first** — anxious users need to parse quickly. Clear hierarchy, measured line lengths, sufficient contrast.
3. **One action at a time** — progressive disclosure over information overload. Show what matters; reveal complexity only when needed.
4. **OKLCH throughout** — perceptually uniform color system, neutrals tinted toward the brand hue.
5. **No AI tells** — no gradient text, no side-stripe card borders, no glassmorphism, no rounded card grids with icon+heading+text.
